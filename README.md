1. THE FIRST LETTER. That all Chriſtians ought to learn of Jeſus Chriſt Meekneſs, Mildneſs, Gentleneſs, and Lowlineſs of Heart.

1. THE II. LETTER. That all Chriſtians ought to learn of Jeſus Chriſt Meekneſs, Lowlineſs and Humility of heart.

1. THE III. LETTER. Men can ſufficiently know the will of God if they would perform it.

1. THE IV. LETTER. Humility is acquired by the knowledge of our ſelves.

1. THE V. LETTER. Humility is acquired by the Knowledge of ones Self.

1. THE VI LETTER. Humility is attained by Knowing our ſelves.

1. THE VII. LETTER. He that ſerves the Luſts of his Senſes, by that ſame Renounces the Love of God.

1. THE VIII. LETTER. It is eaſier to love God than any other Thing.

1. THE IX. LETTER. That it is eaſier to Love God than any thing elſe.

1. THE X. LETTER. The Love of God is eaſie, and renders all things eaſie.

1. THE XI. LETTER. We muſt always Advance in Virtue, whatever Impediments come in the way.

1. THE XII. LETTER. The Devil, the Enemy of true Virtue, Oppoſes it with all manner of Devices.

1. THE XIII. LETTER. Whatever is Senſible to Nature, is not the Work of Grace or Virtue.

1. THE XIV. LETTER. The Devil Transforms himſelf into an Angel of Light.

1. THE XV. LETTER. The Devil incites to Good, that he may bring Evil out of it.

1. THE XVI. LETTER. The Devil Corrupts our Good Works.

1. THE XVII. LETTER. Sin proceeds from Man's Freewill.

1. THE XVIII. LETTER. The Good and Evil Spirit are known by their Fruit.

1. THE XIX. LETTER. We cannot, in this Life, be Free from Tentations.

1. THE XX. LETTER Spiritual Diligence is neceſſary to Salvation.

    _ LETTER I. Of Toleration; That it ought never to go the length of communicating with Sin, directly, or indirectly. Of the Eſſential, and of the Perfection of Chriſtianity. Of Religions, Worſhips and Ceremonies, and their uſe.

1. Here follow ſome CONFERENCES, Taken out of the THIRD PART Of the LIGHT OF THE WORLD.

    _ CONFERENCE XI. Declares, how we muſt be regenerated to enter into the Kingdom of Heaven: and that we muſt return into the Dependance of God, which is the only thing which God requires eſſentially of Man.

    _ CONFERENCE XII. Declares, that there is but one only thing to be done, to be ſaved.

    _ CONFERENCE XVII. Is a Recollection of all the preceding Matters; their End, and Uſe.

    _ CONFERENCE XXVI. Declareth the Neceſſity of becoming a Child to enter into the Kingdom of Heaven, renouncing all Worldly Wiſdom, and all Human Abuſes.
